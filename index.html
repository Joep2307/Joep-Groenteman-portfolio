About

I’m Joep Groenteman (He/Him), a fourth year Communication and Multimedia Design student at the HvA. I completed a minor in User Experience Design, and am currently working towards a minor in Urban Interaction Design. I am specifically interested in how people interact with both digital and physical environments.

During my studies, I developed a strong interest and commitment to accessibility and inclusivity, grounded in the belief that digital products should be accessible to everyone.

I am passionate about making sure websites, apps, and public interfaces work well for people with different abilities and needs, and I enjoy finding ways to improve digital experiences so they are accessible for all users.

My studies have instilled a creative, problem-solving way of thinking that pushes me to go beyond the normal boundaries of how things work. For me, it is important to always ask why something exists the way it does, and for whom does it benefit.

I'm always open to diving into new things, especially when it comes to learning and acquiring new skills. I see new project challenges as an opportunity to try something new rather than an obstacle — the best way for me to keep evolving is to stay curious, engage with feedback, and learn from the real world around me.

CASE 02 — NATIONALE NEDERLANDEN · MINOR USER EXPERIENCE DESIGN

Marathon Savings

FigmaConceptingRedesign

Overview

During the User Experience Design minor at HvA, I was tasked with creating a new feature concept for the Nationale Nederlanden app using up to three UX trends: accessibility and inclusivity, gamification, or thoughtful AI. I chose to use gamification to make saving money more accessible for people using the app.

Nationale Nederlanden is well known for sponsoring the Rotterdam Marathon, so I wanted to make use of this while working on the project.

Concept

"Marathon Savings" turns saving into a race-like experience. Everyone runs a marathon at a different pace and has personal goals unique to them — this same idea applies to saving. Some people want to race towards the finish line, others take a more gradual approach to a single goal, while others simply want to see how far they can go.

Process

To make the concept work for different types of savers, I created a variation of the Bartle player types, based on saving goals: target amount and target date.

Marathon Runner: target date and target amount. Sprinter: target amount, no target date. Endurance Runner: target date, no target amount. Interval Runner: no target date or target amount.

Outcome

The result is a savings feature concept that adapts to how each person actually saves, rather than forcing everyone through the same goal-setting flow — while tying naturally into Nationale Nederlanden's Rotterdam Marathon sponsorship.

CASE 03 — ATHORA

European Accessibility Act (EAA)

HTML/CSSAccessibilityTesting

Overview

During my internship at Athora, the parent company of Zwitserleven and Reaal, I conducted an accessibility audit to assess whether the websites were compliant with the upcoming European Accessibility Act '25 (EAA).

Process

I tested the website, mobile app, and PDF documents using screen readers and disability simulation tools, and performed technical checks in the code to identify accessibility issues.

Besides the testing, I created detailed reports and assisted in organizing internal bootcamps for multiple departments to help colleagues understand what accessibility is, why it matters, and what their role is in delivering accessible products.

Outcome

I supported the UX team and copywriters in developing and implementing solutions for the issues I identified, making sure the improvements were actually accessible and could be implemented on the website.

CASE 04 — ZWITSERLEVEN

Zwitserleven Redesign

VueD3.jsDesign ops

Overview

While at Athora, I supported the redesign of the Zwitserleven website using their new design system, and was able to contribute to further development of the design system itself. I worked on redesigning existing pages and creating new pages for new products.

Process

Our goal was to find the right balance between applying the new design while keeping the experience familiar, so customers could still easily find their way around. I was part of a multidisciplinary team with a variety of different roles, including copywriters, developers, and legal stakeholders.

Outcome

I contributed to improving the design system in Figma by helping define components, documenting usage guidelines, researching new components, and supporting the team in applying the system across the website.
