CASE 01 — MINOR URBAN INTERACTION DESIGN

The Line

ResearchUrban DesignPrototype

Overview

During the Urban Interaction Design minor at the HvA, my team and I explored how we could encourage people to break free from their daily routines and experience the city differently. The result was The Line, an urban concept that invites people to discover alternative walking and cycling routes through Amsterdam.

By combining physical interventions with a digital platform, we wanted to make exploration feel playful, accessible, and community driven.

Alternative routes that encourage people to explore beyond their usual path.

Concept

The Line encourages people to follow a coloured line through the city instead of taking the fastest route. Along the way, users discover quieter streets, local businesses, hidden places, and unexpected experiences that they would normally pass by.

Process

The project started with observations and interviews throughout Amsterdam to understand how people move through the city. We noticed that most people repeatedly followed the same routes and rarely explored outside their routines.

Using these insights, we designed and tested several concepts before developing The Line into a complete experience consisting of physical wayfinding, a website, and a platform where users can create and share their own routes.

Outcome

The final concept combines urban design with digital interaction to encourage exploration and social connection. Rather than simply helping people reach a destination, The Line transforms the journey itself into the experience.

The Line website allows users to discover, create, and share their own routes.

CASE 03 — ATHORA

European Accessibility Act (EAA)

HTML/CSSAccessibilityTesting

Overview

During my internship at Athora, the parent company of Zwitserleven and Reaal, I conducted an accessibility audit to assess whether the websites were compliant with the upcoming European Accessibility Act '25 (EAA).

Cover page of one of the WCAG 2.2 audit reports delivered to stakeholders

Process

I tested the website, mobile app, and PDF documents using screen readers and disability simulation tools, and performed technical checks in the code to identify accessibility issues.

Running the Zwitserleven homepage through the WAVE evaluation tool

Checking ARIA attributes and markup directly in the code

Besides the testing, I created detailed reports and assisted in organizing internal bootcamps for multiple departments to help colleagues understand what accessibility is, why it matters, and what their role is in delivering accessible products.

Outcome

I supported the UX team and copywriters in developing and implementing solutions for the issues I identified, making sure the improvements were actually accessible and could be implemented on the website.

EAA bootcamp for developer teams (04.06.2025)
